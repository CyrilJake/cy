Bachelor of Science in Computer Science (BSCS)

The BSCS program equips students with a strong foundation in computing theory and software development. Students learn programming, algorithms, artificial intelligence, machine learning, database systems, and software engineering. Graduates are prepared to pursue careers in software development, research, and emerging technologies.

College of Information Technology (CIT)

The CIT program focuses on applied technology skills, including networking, cybersecurity, database management, and system administration. The course emphasizes hands-on training with industry-standard tools and prepares students for IT support, systems management, and infrastructure development. Graduates are job-ready and capable of managing real-world IT environments.
